Hi there, I'm Max Larter, welcome to my personal website!

I am a plant ecophysiologist and an evolutionary biologist. My primary research interest is understanding how plants adapt to their environment, and in particular when it is rapidly becoming warmer and with radical changes in rainfall patterns. By examining current trait distributions of living and extinct lineages, we can gain insight into how critical physiological traits have evolved in the past, and what genetic mechanisms have enabled that variation.

I use a combination of physiological and morphological information, ecological and climatic data in an evolutionary framework to try and answer these questions.

I'm currently doing a post-doc with Frederic Lens at the Naturalis Biodiversity Center in Leiden (NL). I'm working on drought-resistance and root pressure in herbs. This project will combine classical plant hydraulics adapted to non-woody species (minimum water potential measurments, cavitation resistance, hydraulic conductance) with xylem anatomy (SEM), micro-CT in-vivo visualization and modelling to aim for a holistic vision of herb hydraulic functionning during drought and the potential role of positive root pressure in providing plants with a way to avoid death during drought.

We hope to obtain these data across multiple species within a range of environmental conditions to gain insight into how these traits are coordinated at evolutionary tinescales.
